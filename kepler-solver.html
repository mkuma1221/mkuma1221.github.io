Results

  • The results compare the analytical Kepler solution with the numerical ODE45 propagation for different solver tolerances. The comparison is evaluated using state vectors, specific orbital energy, and specific angular momentum.

ECI State Vector Comparison

The state vector comparison shows strong agreement between the analytical and numerical solutions. As expected, the ODE45 solution with tighter tolerances closely follows the Newton–Raphson Kepler propagation. At higher tolerances, small deviations accumulate over time, particularly in the velocity components, due to numerical integration error in the two-body dynamics.


The specific orbital energy is defined as:

\[ \epsilon(t) = \frac{1}{2}\|\vec{v}(t)\|^2 - \frac{\mu}{\|\vec{r}(t)\|} \]

From the results, the analytical solution maintains a perfectly constant energy, as expected for an ideal two-body system. The ODE45 solutions show slight numerical drift, with larger oscillations observed for the $10^{-2}$ tolerance case. As the tolerance is tightened, the energy conservation improves significantly, confirming increased numerical stability.


The specific angular momentum magnitude is given by:

\[ \|\vec{h}(t)\| = \|\vec{r}(t) \times \vec{v}(t)\| \]

The angular momentum remains nearly constant for the analytical solution, as expected from conservation laws. For the numerical integration, slight deviations appear in the loose tolerance case due to accumulated numerical error. However, for tighter tolerances ($10^{-6}$ and $10^{-10}$), the solution closely preserves angular momentum, confirming the accuracy of the integrator.
